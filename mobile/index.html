Clickmazes Mobile (Bill's page!)

New for June 2012 : Clickmazes BoxUp
for iPhone, iPod Touch and iPad

Ever since Apple introduced the iPhone we have faced regular requests, from fans of Clickmazes, to develop the puzzles for mobile devices. Wriggle was developed for the App Store in 2009 by Conlan Rios in collaboration with Andrea and Tom Jolly, and has already proven enormously popular.  I (Bill) have now retired from the day-job, which has given me time to take up the challenge of app development for myself. As a result, we have just published another clickmazes puzzle on the App Store store, this time it's the BoxUp puzzle. The Clickmazes BoxUp app has been developed entirely in-house at clickmazes by Bill and Andrea, with puzzle layouts by Bob Henderson.

There's over a hundred puzzles to solve in the BoxUp app, and we've included some unique and novel features, such as an animated puzzle solver. The solver allows you to view the solutions of puzzles, even those that you have not yet solved, and that's not cheating! If you view the solution of an unsolved puzzle you will, however, be prevented from attempting the puzzle yourself until a period of five minutes has passed. If you are able to memorise the entire solution, and to recall it five minutes later, then you will be awarded a full score. But don't assume that'll be easy!

Good news for iPad users

We don't think iPad owners will be disappointed as BoxUp has been designed from the outset not just for iPhone and iPod Touch, but also for iPad.  All BoxUp screens support auto rotation on iPad, for example, with appropriate adjustments to the layout to make best use of whichever orientation is preferred.  BoxUp also uses iPad 'pop overs' (partial screen overlays) to avoid the tiresome and unnecessary screen switching that is often associated with apps that treated iPad as an afterthought.

A little tip from Bill

I personally recommend that you take some time, when getting started with Clickmazes BoxUp, to view a few of the puzzle solutions. The longer ones are great fun to watch, plus you'll get to see some of the tricks and tactics that are necessary when solving them for yourself.  Just select a puzzle from the list, press the i button to be taken to the information screen, then press the Optimum Solution button.
